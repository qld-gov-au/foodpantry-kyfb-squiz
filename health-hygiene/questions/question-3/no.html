Warning!

Hand washing is very important to stop food poisoning. Food handlers must wash their hands:

  • when their hands may be contaminated (e.g.: after handling raw meat, after taking out the rubbish)
  • before handling food
  • after using the toilet, smoking, coughing, sneezing, eating, drinking or touching their face or head.

Gloves must be changed between tasks where you would normally wash your hands.

Resources on how and when to wash hands are included in the food safety resource kits at the end of the topic. We recommend you share these resources with your staff.
